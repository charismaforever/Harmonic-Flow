Circle of Fifths

The Circle of Fifths organizes all 12 pitch classes by ascending perfect fifths (or descending perfect fourths). Each step clockwise adds one sharp; each step counter-clockwise adds one flat. Keys adjacent on the circle share the most notes — they are closely related harmonically.

Adjacent keys share 6 of 7 scale tones
△

Coltrane's Giant Steps

John Coltrane's 1959 "Giant Steps" revolutionized jazz harmony. His matrix divides the octave into three equal parts (major thirds: B → G → E♭), creating a symmetrical cycle. Coltrane navigated these distant key centers at rapid tempo — a technique called "Coltrane changes."

Major thirds divide the octave symmetrically into 3

Frequency & Spectrum

Musical notes correspond to specific frequencies (Hz). A4 = 440 Hz by modern standard. Each octave doubles the frequency. The audible spectrum spans roughly 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz. The harmonic overtone series — multiples of the fundamental — gives each instrument its unique timbre.

A4 = 440 Hz · Each octave = ×2 frequency
∿

Waveform Types

Sine waves produce pure tones with no harmonics. Triangle waves add odd harmonics softly. Sawtooth waves contain all harmonics (odd + even) and sound bright/buzzy. Square waves contain only odd harmonics, producing a hollow, reedy sound. Real instruments combine thousands of partials.

Timbre = the shape of the waveform over time
♩

Chord Theory

Chords are built by stacking thirds. A major triad: root + major third (4 semitones) + minor third (3 semitones). A minor triad: root + minor third + major third. Seventh chords add another third on top. The quality of intervals between notes defines the chord's emotional character.

Major = bright · Minor = dark · Diminished = tense
≋

Modal Scales

The seven modes derive from the major scale starting on different degrees. Ionian (major) sounds happy; Dorian sounds jazzy-minor; Phrygian sounds Spanish/dark; Lydian sounds ethereal; Mixolydian sounds bluesy; Aeolian (natural minor) sounds sad; Locrian sounds unstable/dissonant.

7 modes · 12 keys · 84 possible modal scales
